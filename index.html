A very, very warm (and humid) welcome!

Are you looking for a peaceful picnic spot? Looking to swap the urban jungle for the real one for an afternoon? Welcome then, to the Singapore Botanic Gardens, folks! Where better to have a picnic, than at a UNESCO World Heritage Site? With over a 150 years of history (since 1859, to be precise), name me a better place to have a traditional picnic with your family, than at our very own Gardens, one of the only three botanic gardens inscribed in the world! As you chew on your vegetable crackers and sip on some chamomile tea, simply take in the lush, breathtaking scenery that you dashed out of the office for! Fret not, there's more to the Gardens than just drinking iced tea and staring at trees! Continue reading to find out more!


Heritage Tour

Learn more about the rich history of the Gardens as you walk through the Heritage Core of the Gardens. Admire the various artifacts amidst the wonderful scenery on this guided tour, which repeats every 4th Saturday of the month! Nature admirers will certainly not want to miss a golden opportunity to examine the iconic landmarks of the Gardens. This popular tour features the Bandstand, sculptures in the Gardens, the Heritage Trees, the Main Gate and the Saraca Stream Walk. Do register 15mins before the scheduled tour!

Date Time Venue
Saturday, 23 Jan 9am Visitor Services Counter, Level 1 of Botany Centre

Heritage Museum

The 240sqm museum is located in Holttum Hall, beside the Botany Centre in the Tanglin Core of the Gardens. Child-friendly features include interactive and multimedia exhibits and panels that detail the Gardens' rich heritage. Travel as far back as to the early 19th century, with old photographs, artefacts, plant specimens, rare botanical books and botanical paintings on show. Oral recordings of Ridley during his 100th birthday will also be integrated into an interactive display!

Opening Hours Closed
Mon-Sun, 9am Every last Monday of the month

Jacob Ballas Children’s Garden

The Jacob Ballas Children’s Garden offers children a space for exploration, adventure and play, with a farm, an orchard, and a forest with its own stream and ponds. Young thrill seekers can explore the suspension bridge and nature play areas while budding naturalists can walk along the stream and enter the treehouses. Lastly, aspiring gardeners can learn how plants grow and pick up some gardening tips! Jacob Ballas Children’s Garden is designed for children aged 14 and below, and children aged 12 and below must be accompanied by an adult.

Opening Hours Admission
8am-7pm (Closed on Mondays, except public holidays) Free-of-charge
